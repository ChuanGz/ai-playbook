# Contributing

Contributions must improve the playbook for engineers making or validating AI system decisions and remain aligned with its scope.

## Before implementation

Open an issue before significant work. Describe the concrete engineering problem, expected decision or outcome, evidence, alternatives, and proposed scope. Wait for maintainer agreement before implementation. Small typo or link corrections may proceed directly when clearly safe.

Do not add content primarily to showcase a pattern, technology, vendor, or personal expertise. New guidance must address an evidenced failure, decision, or operational need. Generic software-engineering material belongs elsewhere.

## Pull requests

Keep changes focused and link the accepted issue. Classify the contribution as a principle, contextual recommendation, reference contract, or checklist control. Explain its evidence, boundaries, tradeoffs, validation, and compatibility impact. Preserve source provenance and identify any guidance the change supersedes.

Documentation-only changes must remain reviewable without requiring access to a private repository, private document, or undisclosed organizational policy.

## Quality standard

Use US English and repository Markdown conventions. Each control needs an observable pass condition. Do not claim production readiness, scalability, security, or effectiveness without defined context and evidence. Run Markdown lint and link checks, review the changed content for secrets and unsupported claims, and update affected navigation.

## Breaking changes

Discuss breaking terminology or contract changes before implementation. Document affected readers, migration steps, alternatives, and rollback considerations.

## Review and decisions

Address feedback constructively. Maintainers may close work that is unclear, inactive, duplicate, out of scope, low quality, or inconsistent with project goals. Maintainers retain final authority over scope, direction, review, and acceptance while applying standards respectfully and consistently.

All participants must follow [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
