# AI Role Map

Titles vary by organization. Use primary decision scope, expected deliverables, and demonstrated outcomes as the canonical distinction.

| Role | Primary scope | Expected deliverables | Core competencies | Typical next scope |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI Engineer | Features and services | Evaluated feature, integration contract, runbook | AI integration, evaluation, software delivery, operations | Senior AI Engineer |
| Senior AI Engineer | Systems and team outcomes | System design, quality baseline, reusable practice | System design, risk analysis, evaluation strategy, mentoring | Lead AI Engineer or AI Tech Lead |
| Lead AI Engineer | Team and cross-service delivery | Delivery plan, engineering standard, adoption evidence | Technical direction, planning, review systems, stakeholder alignment | AI Architect or AI Engineering Manager |
| AI Tech Lead | Product-team execution | Technical plan, review evidence, delivery health | Decomposition, coaching, incident learning, technical leadership | Lead AI Engineer or AI Architect |
| AI Product Engineer | User outcome and product iteration | Experiment, evaluated workflow, product telemetry | Discovery, rapid delivery, evaluation, product judgment | Senior AI Product Engineer or AI Tech Lead |
| AI Platform Engineer | Shared platform and developer experience | Platform contract, SLO, adoption and unit-cost evidence | Platform architecture, reliability, security, enablement | Lead AI Platform Engineer or AI Architect |
| AI Solution Architect | Solution and stakeholder alignment | Solution blueprint, options assessment, acceptance model | Requirements framing, integration, cost and risk trade-offs | AI Architect |
| AI Architect | Cross-system target state | Target architecture, principles, governance mechanism, evolution roadmap | Cross-system design, governance alignment, economics, influence | Principal AI Architect |
| AI Engineering Manager | People, team, and delivery system | Competency plan, ownership model, delivery scorecard, capability evidence | People leadership, capacity design, delivery governance, organizational learning | Director of AI Engineering |

## Shared readiness contract

Every role must be able to:

- frame work in terms of user or business outcomes, constraints, and failure impact;
- distinguish model behavior from deterministic system guarantees;
- make trade-offs across quality, latency, cost, risk, maintainability, and optionality;
- produce evaluation, traceability, security, and operational evidence appropriate to consequence;
- explain rejected alternatives, failures, corrections, and personal contribution;
- improve other engineers through review, documentation, mentoring, or reusable mechanisms.

## Readiness review

A credible case includes at least one real decision, one rejected alternative, one evaluation design, one failure or changed assumption, and one measured outcome. Polished output without baseline, ownership, trade-offs, or operational evidence does not demonstrate role readiness.
