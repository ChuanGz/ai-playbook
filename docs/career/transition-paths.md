# Career Transition Paths

Use the closest path and close only the evidence gaps. Existing engineering or leadership seniority remains valuable, but it does not automatically prove AI-specific scope.

## Fresher to AI Engineer

Priority gaps: AI limitations, context and grounding, evaluation, approval, traceability, privacy, monitoring, and cost awareness.

1. Build one bounded feature from an explicit acceptance contract.
2. Create normal, edge, unanswerable, and unsafe evaluation cases.
3. Add observability, escalation, and failure handling.
4. Improve the feature from evidence.
5. Present decisions and personal contribution in a concise case study.

**Required evidence:** working feature, tests, evaluation results, decision record, known limitations, runbook, and reviewed case study.

## Software Engineer to AI Engineer

Preserve system design, implementation, testing, security, operations, debugging, and team-delivery evidence. Close gaps in probabilistic behavior, context engineering, AI evaluation, configuration traceability, human oversight, and model-quality/cost/latency trade-offs.

**Required evidence:** a production-relevant capability, evaluation report, architecture decision, operational evidence, and measured improvement over a baseline.

## Senior Engineer to AI Architect

Close gaps in adoption framing, solution options, AI runtime boundaries, evaluation and governance alignment, target-state evolution, ownership, economics, and cross-team influence.

1. Lead an assessment with measurable outcomes and constraints.
2. Produce credible options, trade-offs, target architecture, and transition decisions.
3. Connect policy to delivery and runtime mechanisms.
4. Support a pilot through a scale, revise, or stop review.
5. Demonstrate reuse across multiple teams or systems.

**Required evidence:** assessment, options analysis, target architecture, decision records, capability roadmap, pilot scorecard, and adoption without continual author support.

## Engineering Manager to AI Engineering Manager

Preserve hiring, coaching, performance management, capacity planning, stakeholder alignment, and delivery accountability. Close gaps in AI competency design, evaluation-driven acceptance, uncertainty management, human oversight, ownership boundaries, and quality/cost/risk/adoption metrics.

**Required evidence:** competency plan, ownership model, delivery scorecard, risk review, incident lesson, and measurable team outcome. Procurement and certificate counts are not management outcomes.
